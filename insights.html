Latest Regulatory Changes
What's changed recently in EU law
Key updates that affect Indian textile and leather exporters.

EUDR — Compliance deadline for large companies: Dec 2025

The EU Deforestation Regulation requires leather exporters to provide full supply chain traceability proving products are deforestation-free. SMEs must comply by June 2026.

EU Textile EPR — Entered into force Oct 2025

The revised Waste Framework Directive introduces mandatory Extended Producer Responsibility for textiles. EU member states have 20 months to implement national schemes. Eco-modulated fees will penalise less durable or recyclable products.

CSDDD — Transposition into national law underway

EU member states are transposing CSDDD into national law (deadline: mid-2026). Textiles is named as a "high impact" sector. EU buyers will be legally required to audit their Indian suppliers for human rights and environmental compliance.

ESPR / Digital Product Passport — Delegated acts in progress

The European Commission is developing specific rules for textiles under ESPR. Digital Product Passports expected to become mandatory for apparel by 2027. Products without a DPP won't be allowed on the EU market.
