⚠️ Mangrove — Structural Difference Not in Model

From Q9, Line 157 — directly from document

What the document says (Q9, Line 157): "While agroforestry projects can be developed on lands owned by small farmers, mangroves are generally on government/community lands, which require their active participation."

What this means for the model: The current model treats all three project types identically for cost structure — individual farmers with land documents, mobilized by the NGO. For mangrove projects, this is wrong. Mangrove land is typically government or community-owned, meaning:
 • Farmer onboarding costs are structured differently — it's community/panchayat engagement, not individual farmer enrollment
 • Regulatory approvals (coastal zone regulations, forest department clearance) are required — currently not in any cost line
 • Revenue sharing flows to communities/government, not individual farmers
 • The mobilization and training cost model ($10/farmer, 0.5 farmers/ha) does not apply

Model gap: Mangrove financial output should be treated as directional only. A separate cost structure would be needed before using it for actual investment decisions.
